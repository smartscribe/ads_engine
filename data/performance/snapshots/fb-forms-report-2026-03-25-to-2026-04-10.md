# FB Forms Experiment — Snapshot Log

## Purpose of experiment
Determine top-of-funnel economics for Meta lead forms. Scale-up gate: LTV/CAC ≥ 1.4.

## Funnel
117 leads → 70 called → 21 contacted → 21 meetings → 4 trialing → 0 active. $0 MRR.

## Cost
Total spend $4,845.11 ($3,518 lead-gen campaigns + SDR labor $1,276). CPL $50.99. Worked-leads cost $3,569 (70 × $50.99). No MRR to date.

## Speed-to-lead (Jack calling same day)
Mar 9–15: 413h → Mar 30–Apr 5: 30h → Apr 6–12: 22h. 95% improvement.

## Quality mix (all 117 leads)
- Solo 55%, Small (2–5) 23%, Mid/Large/Enterprise 20%
- Clinician 50%, Owner/CEO 38%, Admin 2%, Medical Director 2%

## Meeting mix (21 meetings)
- Solo 71%, Small 14%, Mid 5%, Large 10%
- Owner/CEO 62% of meetings vs 38% of leads — **Owners book at ~2× the rate of clinicians**
- Big-practice pipeline names: Tunde Ali (15 clinicians), Laura Dee / Nexus (35), Louise C. / HIA (50) — not converted yet

## Watch items from Chris
- Show rate < 50%
- Too many solo practitioners and non-psychiatrists coming through
- 4 trialing, 0 active — conversion not yet proven
- 3 CCs collected (2 old discount plan, 1 new unlimited-support + 2-templates plan)

## Non-obvious callouts
- **Owner-vs-clinician book-rate gap is the biggest creative lever surfaced.** 2× delta, measurable, actionable via ad copy/targeting bias.
- Lead form CPL ($51) is cheap vs. historical weekly CpFN ($189–$354). Economics only close if trial→active conversion lands.
- 2 JotBill meetings booked from these trialers — implies product-pull beyond SmartScribe, worth tracking separately.
