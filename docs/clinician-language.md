# Clinician Language Guide

Accumulating vocabulary doc. Each term is a real BH-clinician or admin-side term-of-art with provenance. Used by `/landing-page-to-ad-creative` for ad copy and by sales/outreach skills for vocabulary alignment.

**Rules:**
- Every entry has a source citation (landing page, customer quote, regulatory doc, or Nate's confirmation).
- Confirmed-by-Nate entries are the strongest tier; unconfirmed entries are working hypotheses.
- Terms are grouped by topic, not chronologically.

---

## Audit risk and compliance

### SALT / Same-As-Last-Time
**Definition:** The documentation pattern that emerges when an EHR makes copy-forward easy and the clinician runs out of time to differentiate the new note. Named by clinic admins, not by vendors. Triggers OIG cloned-documentation review.
**Source:** never-salt-again landing page, 2026-05-04. Confirmed by Nate.
**Use in copy:** Owns this acronym. Use as-is, no need to expand on second reference inside an ad.

### Clawback / Recoupment
**Definition:** When a payer takes back what they already paid, often with interest. Distinct from claim denial (which never gets paid in the first place). In BH, recoupment is the standard payer response to cloned-documentation findings.
**Source:** never-salt-again landing page, 2026-05-04 (T.P., LCSW quote: "the clawback was hundreds of thousands of dollars"). Confirmed by Nate.
**Use in copy:** "Clawback" is the clinician-side casual term; "recoupment" is the formal/regulatory term. Use clawback in headlines, recoupment in evidence/footers.
